The Seven Princesses: A Presentation of Nezami’s Poem

1.      Introduction

This presentation of Nezami’s poem, The Seven Princesses (the prototype title  is Haft Peykar and it means The Seven Beautiful Women), is based on the review written by Italo Calvino in his book Perche leggere i classici, which informs us in great detail about the story and its narrative methods. The Seven Princesses was published in the Italian newspaper La Republica (8th April 1982), titled Sette Spose per Sette Nouvelle and it is a Persian poem of 12th century, which reflects the habit and the custom of polygamy, an attitude affecting the narrative methods used by the poet, according to Italo Calvino. The aim of this presentation is to inform the readers about the complicated narrative methods this poem has and how interesting are· in addition, it will be made an effort to show the main characteristics of the Oriental Literature.

 Before we start the analysis of the text, it is considered necessary to be pointed out that this classic work of the Persian Medieval Literature has only one translation in Italian, written by Alessandro Baouzani and edited by the editions Rizzoli (the same translation was edited with less notes by the editorial house Leonardo da Vinci). There is only one English translation (written in 1924), which has a great deal of misunderstandings, the translation in Germany is extremely free in some parts of the poem and a French translation does not exist.

 

2.      The main characteristics of the poem

2.1 The plot of the poem

The plot of the poem is the following: there is a king, named Bahram, who falls in love with seven princesses at the same time, looking their portraits. He gets married with all of them and each and every of them lives in a tent· each tent has its own color and it symbolizes a continent and a planet. The king makes a weekly visit in each of his wives and he hears a story. These seven stories are fairy-tales, which have admirable and sometimes odd events presented, like those in the very famous and popular work, A Thousand and One Nights, one of the most remarkable stories in the Oriental Literature. The important is that these stories have a didactic and moral meaning, although this message is not always easy to be known, because of the symbolic character these stories have. Hence, these weekly visits make the king wealthier in the didactic messages received and thus, he can learn about the human ethics, what is or must be moral or immoral, good or bad. So, the pedagogical character this work has is undoubted.

 

2.2 The language techniques and their effects

One the most crucial techniques is the connection between the color and the deep meanings of every story. On the whole, the colors play an important role in these stories, as it has already been mentioned above, and their symbolic value is determinative in understanding the moral meanings of these stories. So, the colors are connected with the deeper meanings of the stories and it is essential to be understood by the readers, in order to have a complete knowledge of the story and not face it as a simple myth.

Furthermore, it must be mentioned that the colors are connected with the metaphors in the texts· metaphors, as a way of literature expression, have a crucial role in the text and they are exceptional symbols of various things. For instance, a multi-dynamic metaphorical element is the cypress· it symbolizes the man’s beauty and sexual power and the ideal feminine model of beauty, which the main characteristic is the height, or it may be connected with the woman’s hair. These symbols are classical motifs started from the Classical Literature. As an example, it can be refereed that Anchises understands that the woman with whom has made love is goodness, when Aphrodite stands up and he notices her height. The same symbols may have the light candle. So, it is concluded that one thing may have different symbols in different contexts or even at the same time and as a result, it is created a net of metaphors and symbols, which makes both the structure and the meaning of the text more complicated.

 

3.      The narrative methods of the poem

As far as it concerns the narrative methods of the poem, these are complicated because of the multiply meanings presented of all these stories. The seven stories contain love adventures presented in a multiply form, which is not typical in the Literature of West and its models. This is expected, according to Calvino, because the multiplicity is connected with the custom of polygamy in these societies, as it is mentioned in the introduction of our article.

            The typical narrative frame is that the hero passes through many stages and if he deals with all these adventures and difficulties successfully, he will be able to marry his love and gain the throne. In contrast, in this story, we see a hero who in each adventure he faces up to, he gains a new bride and this new wife does not excludes the next to come. Hence, the writer of the poem success two things: firstly, he creates a hero rich in experience and knowledge in all levels and secondly, his work reflects the custom of polygamy, that is the social circumstances his society has, if we have in our mind that Nezami (1141-1204) was born and died in Gantza, an area in today’s Azerbaijan. Hence, the population there was a mixture of various peoples and tribes of the East, a fact that affected the poem without any doubt. Hence, the poem reflects the multiculturalism of the poet’s society and the wide range of traditions, habits, ideologies and customs, with characteristic examples the Islamic mysticism and the zoroastrical Persia of the long past. What’s more, the poem exhibits the will of the Gods, which must be accepted by the human beings, but also the life of the real world. It must also be pointed out that the poem is affected by the different religious and religion beliefs existed in poet’s society and among them there is Christianity, too. This variety of traditions is unique and complicates the narration and the meanings of the text.

            Except from the multicultural significance of the poem, it is also important the creation of the portrait of the ideal chief. This is a usual motif which starts from the Classical (Ancient Greek and Latin) Literature in West. It is widely known that Ancient Greek historians, especially Thucydides and Xenophon, provide the ideal chief. Particularly for Xenophon, his model was Agisilaos, the king of Sparta. Hence, the king Bahram symbolizes the human being in the superior degree. This human has a cosmic harmony, reflected in his country, his kingdom, his nation, his personality and way of life.

 

4.      The main narrative techniques

To summarize, this poem contains a great deal of various traditions with many differences among them and its multiculturalism is reflected in the difficult language and the narrative multiplicity. There are seven stories, full of narrative techniques, metaphors and symbols, presented moral qualities a human must try to obtain in his life. The researchers believe that this poem has two models of narrations: the epic type, which is also in the work of Ferntousi, The Book of the Kings, a source of inspiration for Nezami, and the narration type from the ancient collections of India, which is a great pleasure for the readers.

Summarizing the aspects mentioned, it must be remarked that this complicated poem presents many cultural elements, smells and perfumes from the Oriental World. The space of the text has a love tension, is dreaming and rich in psychological alterations, which make it unique and reflect the polygamy of the Oriental World in the best way.
