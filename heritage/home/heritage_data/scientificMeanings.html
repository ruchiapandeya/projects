Literature, Philology, Linguistics: Scientific meanings & objects for research.

 

1.      Introduction

Every scientific sector and sector of knowledge needs rethinking. So does the scientific community with the Science of Speech. So often the rethinking in the Science of Speech has become a way of critical thinking which is interested in analyzing, describing and maybe, judging a text or a corpus of texts. There are also many other kinds of rethinking in the Science of Speech, such as the History of the Literature, the Sociology of the Literature etc.[1]

            So, the aim of this article is to explain some scientific meanings which are thought to have been confused and help us understand the differences and the similarities the Sciences of Speech have and their relations with other kinds of Art, because the they are believed to be the study of the Art of Language.

 

2.      Grammatology, Literature, Philology: Different meanings, but so connected

 

2.1  The definition of Grammatology

Grammatology is the science which examines every kind of written text and especially, the texts which have an aesthetic value and they are considered to be literature in any spoken or non-spoken / “dead” language in the world. These texts are examined analytically and synthetically, statically and dynamically, each and every of them or all together or in combinations, in order to be found the relations between them, diachronically or synchronically. The aim of that study of the texts, which are found or are going to be found in the near future in papyrus, manuscripts or even in stones, wood and other material, is to find the laws which define the production, the structure, the function and the effect of these texts in the human society, the way of thinking, the attitude and on the whole, the history and life. So, Grammatology is considered to be one of the most significant sciences of human and his civilization (sciences humaines).[2]

 

2.2  The definition of the Literature & its connection with Philology

Form the definition of the science of Grammatology above, it has been born the question what is Literature and how it can be defined. Actually, this is a question, which cannot be answered, according to my personal opinion, because of its lack of stability. Being more specific, it is thought that Literature is defined differently in each and every culture and era. Thus, what text or kind of texts are thought to be literature and have this aesthetic value and characteristics is difficult to be defined. There are no specific rules and stereotypes, but the criterion of what is considered literature or not is differentiated in each time and culture, relatively with a great deal of factors. So, the simplest definition which can be given in this case is that Literature is the corpus of some texts which are thought to have aesthetic value and the characteristics thought to be literature, as these have been defined in each and every era and culture. In conclusion, it is understood from the information and the definitions given, that Literature is a part of the Science of Grammatology, as Grammatology studies not only the literature texts, but also the texts which are simpler and have a practical use in the everyday life of the humanity.[3]

            Another important thing which must be mentioned is the definition of the text. Text is thought to be a system of written linguistic points, organized in a structure of a definite size and it has at least one kind of information, which is referred to the actual reality.[4]

            On the other hand, if this text has aesthetic value and it is considered to be literature, it is called work. The definition of the work means the structure and the structural organization of a text, but also the ars, that is the aesthetic creation and characteristics the text has. An undoubted characteristic a literature text or corpus of texts has is their written form. Being written a literature text means that it is incorporated in the history and the civilization of a population or a community. This incorporation has as a consequence the liberation of the text from its writer / creator and the specific time of its creation. The affect of this double liberation is the new function a literature text,-or better,- work may have and that is, its aesthetic value.[5]

            In any case, the important point which should be in our mind is that the Science of Grammatology has been founded in Europe in the first decades of the 19th century, but it has a very long history from the Classical, Hellenistic and Roman era and culture, too. So, there have been developed three directions: a) philology (φιλολογία), b) writing down the texts (καταγραφή) (catalogues of texts, biographies) and c) the way of writing poetry and on the whole, a text (Ποιητική) (the Theory of Literature, which is continued to exist and being developed in our era, too).

            From these three directions, it must be mentioned the Science of Philology (φιλολογία). Philology is the science which has as object of studying the literature texts and what is called and defined literature in any era and culture in the world and in many languages∙ in addition to this, the main studying object of Philology is the critical editing of the texts, the study of language and its development, the morphology or grammar and the syntax and their function in the total of the text.[6]

            Without any doubt, important is also considered the Theory of Literature, the so-called Ποιητική, is defined as the total of aesthetic rules and laws which drive a writer to create his or her own work. These aesthetic rules may be strict and created by a specific School, as there are many of them (the Romantic School, the Russian Formalism, the Structuralism, the New Criticism).[7] The most recently used theories of writing and analyzing a text is a great variety of linguistic models, such as the structural or the genetic model. This proofs that the science of Linguistics has been developed in the past years, as Linguistics is a young science and it is used as a “pilot” in order to “explore” and create a text.[8] Hence, it is essential to define what Linguistics is and their role in the Science of Grammatology and generally, the Sciences of Speech.

 

3.      Language & Linguistics

 

3.1  The definition of the Language

In order to define the Science of Linguistics, it is an urgent need to define its object of studying and researching, that is language. It is widely known how difficult and confusing is to define what language is or may be. When we usually speak about language, it is meant the natural, human language which comes in contrast with the language of the animals∙ however strange as it may seems, animals have their own language, that is a specific and limited code of communication in order to exchange some information (e.g. where the food is).[9] However, the human language is much more complicated and difficult to be understood∙ its main characteristics are the multiplicity and the flexibility, that is the human language can express many meanings in different ways or the same meaning in the same way or different meanings in one way and there is a great deal of information and messages that can be expressed and transferred with it.

            Language is something that we know, we obtain as times goes by and with the personal effort and practice, an action (speaking, writing), an ability, a kind of social behavior, an event, an object (the structure and the way the language functions), an organism. So, we will support the aspect that language is each and every of these definitions and all of them, too. However, the most significant definition is considered to be this told by Saussure (1979,45), who defined the language as a system of symbols which express our thoughts, opinions, ideas, emotions, knowledge, information and exchange all of them with the other members of the community of the language. Hence, language is a phonetic and acoustic system.[10] So, the most significant function of the language is thought to be the communication among the people-members of a community of a language, who speak the same language and can communicate with each other.[11]

           

3.2  The definition of the Science of Linguistics

From the definition of the language expressed above, it is understood that Linguistics is a Science of the Speech which studies the phenomenon of the language, what language is, how it affects the function of the brain, how it functions on the whole, how and why it affects human life, what is and if there is a connection with the human nature. Linguistics studies the language as an internal system (λόγος) and an application of this system in the speakers (ομιλία). As we have already understood, language is heterogenic, that is it’s not the same, but it differentiates under various circumstances· these differentiations are the studying objects of the Linguistics.

            Linguistics is an autonomous sector of the Sciences of Speech, but it has always been fallen into the Science of Philology, because its main studying object is language. So, Linguistics is used to study and analyze the literature from the aspect of the function of the language in the frame of a text.

 

4.      Sciences of Speech & other Arts

4.1  Philology & Music

As it is said above, Philology uses Linguistics in order to analyze the texts· language is said to be a phonetic and acoustic system of symbols, which have a specific meaning, so language has a connection with music. This connection is thought to be indispensable and there are many who are in accordance with this aspect. Even a very developed musical system can be connected with the literature, as it has been proved from the Lied and the opera. Furthermore, in Ancient Greece, the lyric poetry was sung, the epic poetry was told in a rhythmic way, -something like the today’s opera, - and the dramatic poetry was a synthetic spectacular, combining language and rhythm, music and dance. In addition, a music work can be recreated in a literature way and the affections between both arts is without any doubt.[12]

 

4.2  Literature & Cinema

The art of film and the literature have many similarities and there can be found many connections between them. The simplest thing can be supported is some similar techniques used, such as the technique of flash-back, which was used in Odyssey. In any way, it must not be forgotten that a film is based in a script, a written text, so the similarities are expected and do not make any sense. Hence, the art of the cinema is a different language, which is considered a rich and synthetic code of communication.[13]

 

4.3  Literature & Painting

The connections between both these arts are common, too. Traditionally, the words of Orator ut pictura poesis (De Arte Poetica, 361) show the connections between these two arts and their function. However, there are different points between them: painting is static and authentically optic, because it expresses the beauty of the object and the body· despite that, literature is based in the function of the time, it has a dynamic and it is narrative and for that reason, is it has action, passion and emotion. This aspect is supported by Lessing. We think that it is partly correct, because the time is not always missing in the painting. Time can be expressed and codified in the art of painting, too. What’s more, the way of writing helps the connection between literature and painting, but and other kind of art of this field, like architectonic, sculpture etc.[14]

 

5.      Conclusion

We think that the basic meanings, (what is the Sciences of speech and how they function and what they try to study and search), have been expressed in this article in a quick, but satisfying way.
